Program Overview

Brigades build participatory power in their cities by holding regular civic hack nights and events, advocating for open data, and deploying apps. Brigades celebrate and share their successes with the Brigade network to further the global civic innovation movement.

Who We Are

Code for America believes government can work for the people, by the people in the 21st century.

We build open source technology and organize a network of people dedicated to making government services simple, effective, and easy to use.

Here's how:

  • We encourage and empower residents to take an active role in their community.
  • We facilitate collaboration between government staff and foster forward-thinking approaches to solving city problems.
  • We support civic-minded entrepreneurs and startups.

Brigade's Organize through the Four Ps

  • People. Every Brigade needs leadership, core team members, regular hack nights, and online chanelles where citizens can engage.In Chicago, the Civic User Testing group ensures that new civic apps undergo rigorous user testing with actual citizens from a diverse set of racial, socioeconomic, and geographic backgrounds.
  • Plans. As Brigades build, they set startegic plans organized around the Code for America Principles of Digital Government.
  • Partnerships. One key way Brigades accomplish their goals is by partnering with local government officials and other community organizations in their cities.
  • Participation. Brigades utilize the various Code for America network platforms to share stories, build and re-deploy projects, and further organize around transparency in government.
