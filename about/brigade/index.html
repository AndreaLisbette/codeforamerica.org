Program Overview

Brigades are volunteer organizations that build participatory power in their cities by holding regular civic hack nights and events, advocating for open data, and deploying apps. Brigades celebrate and share their successes with the Brigade network to further the global civic innovation movement.

Who We Are

Brigade Network Members are Brigade's that are meeting regularly, and building ways for community members to share ideas and build solutions.
Official Brigade Chapters evolve as the Brigade members rally behind speific issues and lay out plans for sustained collaboration with other organizations and their local government.
Institutional Brigades are buiiding capacity and scalability, so their Brigade can become its own non-profit organization.

Brigade's Organize through the "Four Ps."


People. Every Brigade needs leadership, core team members, regular hack nights, and online channels where citizens can engage.

Plans. As Brigades build, they set startegic plans that secure alignment around the Code for America Principles of Digital Government.

Partnerships. One key way Brigades accomplish their goals is by partnering with local government officials and other community organizations in their cities.

Participation. Brigades utilize the various Code for America network platforms to share stories, build and re-deploy projects, and further organize around transparency in government.
