Project Summary:

This is a Request for Proposal (“RFP”) for a contractor (the “Contractor”) to provide creative and development services in building a collected set of standardized website design elements - a “pattern library” for the city’s redesigned website, based on the existing Online Visual Identity Guide.

Project Stages:

Work will proceed in three stages:

  • Discovery - Contractor reviews materials, assesses technical options and proposes design approaches (approx. 2 weeks).
  • Design & Development - Contractor builds out design elements in CSS and creates 5-10 sample page types designed to show maximum variety of use. (approx 4-6 weeks)
  • Delivery - Contractor finalizes designs and delivers a complete Pattern Portfolio as an open-source website hosted on Github[b][c].  (approx. 1-2 weeks).

In other words,

This cover page is a simplified summary of all the major terms of the RFP. Sections describe the project, requirements, and application process in a single page.

This section improves typical practice in RFPs by centralizing and condensing all the terms into a concise summary. The intention is to allow potential responders to quickly determine high level requirements and determine whether they should participate in the RFP process. It also significantly streamlines customization - cities simply change the details in this section, rather than having to edit a large number of terms throughout the entire document.

Introduction

The City plans to redesign and enhance its website at URL to provide easier access to City departments and data.

This RFP seeks a Contractor to provide creative services in developing the visual identity of the redesigned website, as a first step to building a collected set of standardized website design elements - a “pattern library” - that will be the subject of a subsequent RFP. For examples of what we hope to achieve, see pattern libraries for Code for America, Mailchimp, and Gov.uk.

Goals

The City aims to achieve the following goals in the new website design:

  • Showcase the City by providing a dynamic website that is [3-4 design values] in appearance.
  • Offer a consistent brand identity for the city’s online communications that is aligned with the existing brand.

In other words,

This section describes the context of the RFP in a single, concise paragraph. It also articulates the goals of the project in two, straightforward bullet points. Cities should aim to clearly articulate a small set of adjectives that give precise direction to businesses making a proposal. Vague design values (e.g. “clean”, “fresh”) will result in less responsive proposals.

Typical RFP practice is to provide long lists of objectives and an extended description of the context of the city website. To improve readability, the OPS approach is to condense this section into a small paragraph and default to a limited number of bullets that express the core needs of the city.
